Common Running Vocabulary


  • Surges: Fast bursts of speed in the middle of a run. Longer and more controlled than strides. Top runners use surges tso break away from the pack.

  • Progression Run: A long run that starts at a slow pace, but gradually picks up in speed each mile.

  • Strides: Fast bursts of speed with recovery in between. Shorter but faster than surges.

  • Fartleks: A workout in which one alternates between a slow pace and a fast pace on a certain interval.

  • Tempo Pace: A fast pace than one can hold for about 4 miles but not much longer.
