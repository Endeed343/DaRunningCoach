How to Get Your Training Plan

  1. Fill out the form

  2. Take a picture of your schedule

  3. After the four weeks of training, you can fill out the form again with your new information if you choose.

1. How many days did you run last week?

2. Are you currently in-season or off-season

Additional Information

 Extra exercises such as hurdle drills and weights are not on the on the schedule, but will certainly compliment your training to make you faster and prevent injury. Use the navigation bar at the top of the page for more information on how to do these extra exercies. Stretches are highly recommended, both before and after your run, so make sure to check out a few examples from this website.

 Your schedule will certainly make you faster, however, every runner is different. Try your best to stick to your schedule, however, if you feel uncomfortable with your pace on easy days, or have an easy time on hard days, you can slightly modify your pace. If you already know your easy, steady, tempo or other paces, you can do those instead of the ones provided. Also, feel free to contact me with any questions!
